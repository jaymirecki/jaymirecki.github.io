Biography

Born in the first days of 1998, Jay Mirecki was a wonderful child. He was loved by his family, despite the expressions on his sister's faces in early family photos. Jay's life, however, has not been without challenges. With his family's support, Jay beat pediatric cancer and survived a fractured skull. Since then, Jay graduated from East Lyme High School and his currently a junior at Tufts University studying Computer Science and Biology.

Jay's goal in life is to see a cure to cancer. In the words of one of his oncologists, "someone will find a cure, why not you?" As his studies in biology continued, Jay realized that there will likely never be one magic bullet to "cure" cancer. The only path to an all purpose solution may be a viral delivery system for a patient's own DNA to replace mutated DNA in cancer cells.

Early in his college career, Jay realized that his best tool in the fight to help pediatric cancer patients may not be a degree in biology, but rather a degree in computer science. Currently enrolled in a machine learning course and looking forward to computational biology in the autumn of 2019, Jay hopes to use the power of computers to help researchers and clinicians deliver the final blow to cancer.
